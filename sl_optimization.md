## Where it falls short

- **EMA crossover lag**: EMA9/21 crossovers are inherently lagging — by the time M5 and M15 EMAs align, a meaningful chunk of the move is often already priced in, especially on a scalping timeframe where every point matters. [in.tradingview](https://in.tradingview.com/scripts/rsi-ema/)
- **Weak in ranging markets**: EMA crossover strategies are "naturally weak in oscillating markets" and prone to frequent false signals when price chops sideways — a real risk on M5 gold/forex charts during low-volatility sessions. [fmz](https://www.fmz.com/lang/en/strategy/486570)
- **RSI thresholds are too close to neutral**: Using 51/47 as buy/sell triggers (rather than classic 60/40 or 70/30) barely filters anything — it's almost indistinguishable from "RSI is on the correct side of 50," which weakens its value as a real confirmation filter. [stockpathshala](https://stockpathshala.com/ema-and-rsi-strategy/)
- **No price-level context**: The logic never checks support/resistance, prior swing highs/lows, or session/liquidity context — it's purely indicator-derived. Most robust MTF frameworks recommend anchoring entries to structural levels, not indicators alone. [ftmo](https://ftmo.com/en/blog/multi-timeframe-range-strategy/)
